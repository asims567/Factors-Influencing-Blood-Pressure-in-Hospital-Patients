# Factors-Influencing-Blood-Pressure-in-Hospital-Patients
This contains the blood pressure dataset, with factors including Sex, Marriage, Smoking, BMI, and Race. This dataset has been provided by the University of Ottawa Statistics course (**HSS 2381**) and the professor. I originally utilized this data set in SPSS for an assignment, but I wanted to repeat this assignment, this time within R software, as it is interchangeable within both platforms. In this dataset, we will analyze the different factors, specifically, **Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP)**, **BMI**, and **Race**, to see the effects on blood pressure. 

Graphs and plots will be showcased to analyze the meaning behind the data set and interpret what it really means in terms of distribution and the effects on blood pressure. 


# **Systolic Blood Pressure - SBP**

Systolic Blood Pressure (SBP) was analyzed using measures of central tendency and variability. The mean, median, and standard deviation were calculated to summarize the distribution of SBP values among patients. A histogram with an overlaid distribution curve was created to assess the shape of the distribution. The distribution appeared to be **left-skewed**.

Due to the skewness in the distribution, the median (**130**) provides a more accurate representation of central tendency than the mean (**125.8**). Therefore, the **median** > **mean**.

# **Body Mass Index (BMI)**
BMI was analyzed similarly using mean, median, and standard deviation. A histogram was used to visualize the distribution. The distribution of BMI appears approximately normal based on the histogram and density curve. 

The shape being a bell curve indicates that the **mean (28.68) and median (26.60) are roughly equal**.

# **Race (RACE)**
Race is a categorical variable, so frequency counts were used to summarize the data. A bar chart was created to display the distribution of patients across racial categories (White, Asian, Black, Other). Since RACE is a nominal variable, the mode (most frequent category) is the most appropriate measure of central tendency. The bar chart shows that the majority of the race distribution is WHite.
